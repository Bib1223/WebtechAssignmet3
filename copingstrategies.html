Deep Breathing Exercises

Description: Deep breathing exercises are effective for reducing anxiety. Diaphragmatic breathing involves deep inhalation through the nose and expanding the stomach. Box breathing consists of inhaling, holding, exhaling, and pausing for four counts each. 4-7-8 breathing includes inhaling for four seconds, holding for seven, and exhaling for eight. These techniques promote relaxation and can be practiced daily.

Steps:

  1. Inhale deeply through your nose, allowing your abdomen to rise for a count of four.
  2. Exhale slowly through your mouth for a count of six, feeling your body relax.

Progressive Muscle Relaxation

Description: Progressive Muscle Relaxation (PMR) is a technique that reduces stress by tensing and relaxing muscle groups. It involves inhaling while tensing a muscle and exhaling while releasing the tension, promoting deep relaxation. PMR typically takes 10 to 20 minutes and enhances mental well-being.

Steps:

  1. Tense a specific muscle group, such as your shoulders, for five seconds while inhaling.
  2. Release the tension while exhaling, noticing the difference in relaxation.
